BUNNY CHOW

Bunny Chow (South African street food)

Bunny chow is a popular South African street food that has become an iconic culinary delight. Despite its name, Bunny chow has no actual connection to rabbits. Instead, it is a delicious creation that originated in the Durban Indian community and has since become a beloved fast food item throughout the country. Bunny chow consists of a hollowed-out loaf of unsliced white bread, which is filled to the brim with flavorful curry. The curry can be made with chicken, lamb, or even vegetarian ingredients, such as chickpeas or lentils. The bread acts as a convenient and edible bowl, soaking up the rich and aromatic curry that oozes with spices like curry powder, turmeric, and cumin. Bunny chow is a true fusion of cultures, representing the diverse heritage and culinary influences of South Africa.

The experience of eating Bunny chow is a messy and joyous affair. As you take a bite into the soft and pillowy bread, you are immediately met with the explosion of flavors from the curry filling. The tender chunks of meat or hearty legumes are coated in a robust and fragrant sauce that seeps into every crevice of the bread. The combination of textures is a delight, with the bread offering a comforting contrast to the slightly spicy and aromatic curry. Bunny chow is often enjoyed as a handheld street food, making it perfect for on-the-go eating. Its popularity has extended beyond South Africa, captivating the taste buds of food enthusiasts around the world who seek to experience this unique and mouthwatering delight.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of unsliced white bread
  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken or lamb, cubed
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander), for garnish

Recipe instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add the garlic, curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and coriander. Stir well and cook for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken or lamb pieces to the pan and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes and broth. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded.
  5. While the meat is simmering, prepare the bread by cutting a square or rectangular portion from the loaf and hollowing it out, creating a bread bowl.
  6. Once the meat is cooked, spoon the filling into the hollowed-out bread. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  7. Serve the Bunny Chow warm as a handheld street food snack or as a meal on its own. Enjoy!
